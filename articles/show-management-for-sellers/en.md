# Show Management for Sellers

## What you'll learn

This guide covers the full lifecycle of a Jamble show: from the moment you schedule it to what happens after you end it. You'll learn how to manage your show before, during, and after going live.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- A scheduled show with products added
- Your seller setup complete (payment info, shipping address, email)

## The show lifecycle

Every show goes through these stages:

```
Scheduled → Pre-Show → Live → Ending → Post-Show
```

This article covers what you can do at each stage.

---

## Stage 1: Pre-show (before going live)

After scheduling your show, you can view it from your profile. Tapping on it opens the **show preview** screen.

### What you see on the preview

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  [Cover image]                  │
│  Your show title                │
│  Date and time                  │
│                                 │
│  ADMIN (private)                │
│  Bookmarks: 12  |  Views: 45   │
│                                 │
│  PRODUCTS (3)                   │
│  ┌─────┐ ┌─────┐ ┌─────┐      │
│  │Item1│ │Item2│ │Item3│  [+]  │
│  └─────┘ └─────┘ └─────┘      │
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Admin stats** (only you can see these): how many people bookmarked your show and how many viewed the page
- **Product list**: the items attached to your show. You can add, remove, or reorder products

### What you can do before the show

- **Edit your show** — tap the menu (three dots) to change title, description, notes, tag, cover image, or date/time
- **Add products** — create new listings or import products from past shows
- **Remove products** — swipe on a product to delete it from the show
- **Delete the show** — in the menu, select delete. A confirmation dialog appears: "Are you sure you want to delete this show?"

## Stage 2: Entering the show room

When it's close to showtime, open your show and enter the host screen. You'll see a **countdown** to your scheduled start time.

![show-countdown: Timer display showing 0d 0h 12m 30s until show starts, with Practice mode and Go Live buttons](./assets/mockups/show-management-for-sellers__show-countdown__en.png)

- **Practice mode** — test your camera, audio, and product pinning privately. No one can see you
- **Go Live** — start broadcasting. A confirmation appears: "Start your Show — Once started, people will be able to join you"

### What happens if you're late

- After your scheduled time: the message changes to **"Time to go live!"**
- After 5 minutes late: **"Last chance to join or postpone the show!"** with a countdown
- After 10 minutes late: the countdown disappears. Your show is still on the calendar but you should start immediately or reschedule
- If you don't start at all: the system will eventually remove your show after an extended period

**Don't be late.** Buyers who show up to an empty show won't come back. If you can't make it, reschedule in advance.

## Stage 3: Live show

Once you tap "Go Live" and confirm, your camera turns on and you're broadcasting. Here's what you see on the host screen.

### Host interface overview

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│ #3 R$450 (R$150/item)  [👥 12] │
│                      [⚙] [...] │
│                                 │
│                                 │
│     Your camera feed            │
│                                 │
│                    [🔄 camera]  │
│                                 │
│ ┌─────────────────────────────┐ │
│ │  Chat messages              │ │
│ │  "Quero esse!" - buyer1     │ │
│ │  "R$50!" - buyer2           │ │
│ └─────────────────────────────┘ │
│ [Product list]  [Start sale]    │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Top-left: Sales dashboard**
Shows your running total during the show:
- Number of items sold
- Total revenue in R$
- Average price per item

**Top-right: Audience counter**
A red badge showing how many viewers are watching. Tap it to see the full list of viewers.

**Right side: Camera controls**
- **Flip camera** — switch between front and back camera
- **Settings menu** — access show settings

### Managing products during the show

This is the core of hosting. Your product list appears at the bottom of the screen.

**Pin a product:** Tap a product from your list to pin it on screen. This is what viewers see and can place offers on.

**Start a sale:** Once a product is pinned, tap the action button to start the sale. What happens depends on the sell mode:
- **Real-time offers**: A timer starts and buyers compete by placing offers
- **Sudden Death**: Same as real-time offers but no timer extension when new offers come in
- **Buy It Now**: The product is available for instant purchase at the fixed price

**Move to next product:** After a sale ends, the app suggests the next product in your list. Tap to pin it and continue.

**Unpin a product:** If you want to switch to a different product, tap the unpin button to remove the current one from the screen.

**Run again:** If a product has remaining quantity after a sale, you can run it again with one tap.

### Chat

Viewers can send messages in real time. You'll see the chat at the bottom of your screen. Engage with your audience — greet people by name, answer questions, and build excitement for your products.

### Camera flip

Tap the camera icon to switch between your front camera (your face) and your back camera (to show products up close). Many sellers switch back and forth throughout the show.

## Stage 4: Ending your show

When you're done selling, tap the **back button** or go to the menu and select **"End Show."**

Before ending, Jamble will offer you the option to **raid** another seller's show — sending your viewers to a currently live seller. This is optional but great for the community.

After the raid (or skipping it), tap **"End Show"** to finish your broadcast.

---

## Stage 5: Post-show

After ending, you'll see a **summary** of your show with key stats:
- Total items sold
- Total revenue
- Number of viewers
- Show duration

From here, you can review your orders and start preparing shipments.

---

## Editing a show after scheduling

You can edit a scheduled show by tapping the menu (three dots) on the show preview screen.

**What you can change before going live:**
- Title
- Description and notes
- Tag (category)
- Cover image
- Date and time (with postpone limits — max 3 postponements to later dates)

**What you can change during a live show:**
Very limited. Once live, you can only toggle certain settings (like free shipping). You cannot change the title, date, or tag during a live show.

## Important tips

- **Add all your products before going live.** You can add products during a show, but it's smoother to have everything ready beforehand
- **Use Practice mode before your first few shows.** Even a quick 1-minute practice helps you feel comfortable with the interface
- **Don't go live late.** After 10 minutes past your scheduled time, the show auto-ends. Reschedule if you know you'll be late
- **Keep your audience engaged.** Talk to your viewers, respond to chat, and keep a good pace between products. Dead air loses viewers
- **Pin products in order.** Organize your product list in the order you plan to show them. The app will suggest the next product automatically
- **Watch your sales dashboard.** The real-time counter at the top-left helps you track how your show is going

## Common questions

**Can I add products while I'm live?**
Yes, but it's better to add them beforehand. If you need to add something during the show, you can do it through the product management interface.

**What happens to unsold products after the show?**
They stay in your inventory. You can add them to your next show or sell them in future shows.

**Can I restart a show that I ended?**
No. Once a show is ended, it cannot be restarted. You would need to create a new show.

**How long can a show last?**
There is no strict maximum duration. Shows can run for as long as you want. However, very long shows may see declining viewership.

**Can viewers join after the show has started?**
Yes. Viewers can join at any point while the show is live. New viewers will see the current product being sold.

**What if my internet drops during a show?**
If you lose connection briefly, your show doesn't end immediately. Try to reconnect quickly. If you're disconnected for too long, the show may end automatically.

**Can I see who's watching my show?**
Yes. Tap the viewer count badge at the top-right to see a list of everyone currently in your show.
