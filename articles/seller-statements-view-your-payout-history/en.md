# Seller Statements — View Your Payout History

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how to access and understand your financial history on Jamble. You'll learn how to view your payout records and keep track of all your withdrawals.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- At least one payout completed (to have history to view)

## Step by step

### Step 1: Open your wallet

Go to your profile, then tap **Settings** → **My Wallet**.

### Step 2: Open Payouts History

In the top-right corner of the wallet screen, tap the **clock icon** (next to the help button). This opens the **Payouts History** screen.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  My Wallet              [?] [⏰]│
│                                 │
│  [Your balance sections]        │
│                                 │
│  Tap the clock icon ──────→ [⏰]│
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Step 3: Review your payouts

The Payouts History screen shows all your withdrawal records, with the most recent at the top.

```

│  Payouts History                │

│  Completed         10/03/2026   │
│  ─────────────────────────────  │
│  #PAY-12330        R$ 95,00    │
│  Failed            08/03/2026   │
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

Each payout entry shows four pieces of information:

| Field | What it shows |
|-------|--------------|
| Payout ID | A unique reference number for this withdrawal |
| Amount | The R$ value that was (or attempted to be) transferred |
| Status | The current state: Created, Processing, Pending, Completed, Failed, Canceled |
| Date | When you requested the withdrawal |

### Step 4: Scroll for older records

Scroll down to see older payouts. The list loads more entries as you scroll. You can also **pull down to refresh** the list and see the latest updates.

## Understanding your statements

### Status colors

- **Purple text** (Created, Processing, Pending) — your payout is in progress
- **Green text** (Completed) — money has been deposited in your bank
- **Red text** (Failed, Canceled) — something went wrong or the payout was stopped

### Reading your history

Your Payouts History is your record of every withdrawal you've made. Use it to:
- **Verify deposits** — match Completed payouts against your bank statements
- **Track pending withdrawals** — see if a recent withdrawal is still processing
- **Identify problems** — spot Failed payouts that need attention
- **Keep records** — maintain a history of your earnings from Jamble

## Important tips

- **Check Payouts History after every withdrawal.** Get in the habit of verifying that your withdrawals reach "Completed" status
- **Save your payout IDs.** If you ever need to contact support about a specific withdrawal, having the payout ID makes it much faster to resolve
- **Failed payouts need action.** If you see a "Failed" status, go back to your wallet, check your bank details, and try the withdrawal again
- **Your wallet balance is separate from payout history.** The wallet shows your current available balance. Payouts History shows the record of all past withdrawals

## Common questions

**Can I export my payout history?**
Currently, the payout history is only viewable in the app. For tax or accounting purposes, you can take screenshots of your history or note down the details manually.

**How far back does the history go?**
Your entire payout history since you started selling on Jamble is available. Scroll down to load older records.

**Can I see individual sale details in the payout history?**
Payouts History shows withdrawals, not individual sales. To see details of specific sales, check your orders in **My Sales**.

**Why do I see a "Failed" payout?**
A payout fails when the transfer to your bank couldn't be completed — usually because of incorrect bank details. Check and update your bank information, then request a new withdrawal.

**The amount doesn't match what I expected. Why?**
Payouts transfer your entire available wallet balance at the time of withdrawal. If some orders were still pending when you withdrew, those earnings weren't included. They'll be available in your next withdrawal after the orders are completed.
