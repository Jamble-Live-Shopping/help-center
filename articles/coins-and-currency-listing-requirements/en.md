# Coins and Currency: Listing Requirements

## What you'll learn

This article outlines the mandatory listing requirements for all coins, banknotes, and currency items sold on Jamble. These rules exist to protect both buyers and sellers, ensuring transparency and trust in the category.

## Requirements for all coins and currency listings

### 1. Use real photos

Every listing must include photos of the **actual item you are selling**. Stock photos, catalog images, or photos of a similar item are not allowed.

If you are selling a coin, photograph that specific coin — not a generic image of the same type.

### 2. Include place of origin

State which country the coin or banknote is from. This can go in the title or description.

**Examples:**
- "Moeda de 1 Real - Brasil - 1994"
- "Liberty Dollar - Estados Unidos - 1921"
- "Cédula de 500 Cruzeiros - Brasil - 1990"

### 3. Include the year or time period

Every coin and banknote listing must state the year it was minted or printed. If the exact year is not visible or known, provide the approximate era.

**Examples:**
- Exact: "1922"
- Approximate: "Período Imperial (1822-1889)"
- Range: "Década de 1970"

### 4. Describe the condition

Use Jamble's condition system to describe the state of the item:

| Condition | Coins | Banknotes |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **New with Tags** | Uncirculated, in original mint packaging | Crisp, uncirculated, no folds |
| **New without Tags** | Uncirculated but not in packaging | Uncirculated condition, minor storage marks |
| **Very Good** | Light circulation, nearly all details preserved | Light handling marks, no significant folds |
| **Good** | Moderate wear, main details visible | One or two folds, some wear visible |
| **Satisfactory** | Heavy wear, some details worn smooth | Multiple folds, yellowing, edge wear |

If the coin is in a **professional grading holder (slab)**, mention the grading company and the numeric grade in your title or description.

## Rules for replica and novelty coins

Selling counterfeit currency is strictly prohibited. However, you may sell **replica or novelty coins** if:

1. You clearly state in the listing that the item is a **replica, novelty, or non-legal tender**
2. The item itself is clearly distinguishable from authentic currency — for example:
   - It is physically marked as "COPY" or "REPLICA"
   - It has a different size, color, or material from the original
   - It omits the legal tender denomination

If a replica coin could be confused with authentic currency, it cannot be sold on Jamble.

## Failure to follow requirements

Listings that do not meet these requirements may be:
- Removed by the Jamble team
- Subject to account restrictions

Repeated violations may lead to suspension of selling privileges.

## Quick checklist

Before publishing a coins or currency listing, verify:

- [ ] Photos are of the actual item (no stock photos)
- [ ] Country of origin is stated
- [ ] Year or time period is included
- [ ] Condition is described using Jamble's condition system
- [ ] Any flaws, cleaning, or restoration are disclosed
- [ ] Replicas are clearly marked as such
