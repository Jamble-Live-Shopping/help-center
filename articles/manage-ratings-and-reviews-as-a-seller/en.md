# Manage Ratings and Reviews as a Seller

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how the rating system works on Jamble, how buyers rate their experience with you, and how your ratings affect your seller reputation.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- At least one completed transaction (to receive ratings)

## How ratings work

After a buyer receives their order, they can rate their experience. The rating reflects the overall transaction — product quality, shipping speed, accuracy of the listing, and communication.

Ratings are tied to individual transactions, not to shows. Each completed order gives the buyer an opportunity to rate you.

## Where to see your ratings

Your overall seller rating is visible on your **profile page**. Anyone visiting your profile — buyers, other sellers, and viewers — can see your average rating.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  @yourusername                  │
│  ⭐ 4.8  (127 ratings)          │
│                                 │
│  Followers: 350                 │
│  Shows: 24                      │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

Your rating is an **average** of all ratings you've received. The more transactions you complete successfully, the more ratings contribute to your average — making it more stable over time.

## What buyers rate

When leaving a rating, buyers evaluate the overall experience. Key factors that influence ratings:

- **Product accuracy** — did the item match the description, photos, and stated condition?
- **Shipping speed** — was the package shipped promptly after the sale?
- **Packaging quality** — was the item packed securely and arrived in good condition?
- **Communication** — were you responsive if the buyer had questions?

## How ratings affect your seller reputation

Your rating is one of the first things buyers see when they visit your profile or join your show. A high rating:

- **Builds trust** — new buyers are more likely to place offers and purchase from highly-rated sellers
- **Increases visibility** — sellers with strong ratings and consistent activity are more likely to appear in recommendations
- **Encourages repeat buyers** — satisfied buyers come back for future shows

A low rating can discourage new buyers from purchasing and may affect how often your shows are recommended.

## How to maintain a high rating

### Be accurate in your listings

The most common reason for low ratings is a mismatch between what the buyer expected and what they received.

- **Describe the condition honestly.** If there's a flaw, mention it in the description AND show it in a photo
- **Use accurate sizes.** Double-check measurements and size labels
- **Show the actual item.** Don't use stock photos. Show the real product you're shipping

### Ship quickly

Speed matters. Buyers expect their items to arrive promptly.

- **Generate your shipping label as soon as possible** after the show
- **Drop off the package** at a Correios agency within 1-2 days
- **Don't wait to accumulate orders.** Ship each order as it comes

### Pack with care

A poorly packed item that arrives damaged leads to returns and bad ratings.

- Use appropriate packaging for the item's size and fragility
- Add padding for delicate items
- Seal the package securely

### Communicate when needed

If there's a delay or issue with an order, reach out to the buyer proactively. A quick message explaining the situation goes a long way.

## Important tips

- **Every transaction matters.** Even one low rating when you have few total ratings can significantly affect your average. Consistency is key
- **First impressions count.** New sellers with no ratings need to work extra hard on their first few transactions to build a positive reputation
- **Ratings are permanent.** You cannot delete or dispute individual ratings (unless they violate Jamble's policies). Focus on prevention rather than correction
- **Quality over quantity.** It's better to sell fewer items with excellent service than many items with sloppy fulfillment

## Common questions

**When does a buyer rate me?**
Buyers are prompted to rate after they receive their order and confirm delivery. They can also choose to rate later from their order history.

**Can I see individual ratings or just the average?**
Your profile shows your overall average rating and total number of ratings. Individual transaction ratings are visible in your order details.

**What happens if a buyer gives me an unfair rating?**
If you believe a rating violates Jamble's policies (contains offensive content, is clearly fraudulent, etc.), you can contact support. Legitimate ratings based on buyer experience — even negative ones — cannot be removed.

**Does my rating affect my ability to sell?**
Your rating doesn't prevent you from selling, but it significantly affects buyer trust. Sellers with consistently low ratings may see fewer viewers and buyers in their shows.

**How can I recover from a low rating?**
Focus on delivering excellent experiences in your next transactions. As you accumulate more positive ratings, your average will gradually improve. There is no shortcut — consistent quality is the only path.

**Can buyers leave written reviews?**
Yes. When rating a transaction, buyers can leave a written review along with their star rating. These reviews are visible on your profile in the **"Reviews"** section.
