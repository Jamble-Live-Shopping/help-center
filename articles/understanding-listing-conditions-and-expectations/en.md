# Understanding Listing Conditions and Expectations

## What you'll learn

This guide explains the condition system on Jamble — how to accurately rate the condition of your items, what buyers expect from each condition level, and how to set the right expectations so you avoid returns and disputes.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Items you want to list (it helps to have them in front of you when rating condition)

## Why condition matters

When buyers see your product during a live show, they rely on two things to decide if they want to buy: **your photos** and **the condition you selected**. If the item arrives and doesn't match what you described, the buyer can open a dispute and request a return.

Being accurate about condition isn't just honest — it's practical. Sellers who rate conditions accurately get fewer returns, better reviews, and more repeat buyers.

## The five condition levels

Jamble uses five condition levels. When creating a listing, you choose the one that best describes your item.

### New with Tags

The item is **brand new and has never been worn or used**. The original tags and labels are still attached. The item is in the same condition as when it was purchased from the store.

**What buyers expect:**
- Original tags and labels still attached
- No signs of wear, stains, or damage
- Original packaging (if applicable)

**When to use it:** Only if the item is genuinely unused and still has its original tags. If you removed the tags but never wore the item, use "New without Tags" instead.

### New without Tags

The item is **brand new and has never been worn or used**, but the original tags have been removed. It's in perfect condition — just missing the tags.

**What buyers expect:**
- No signs of wear, stains, or damage
- In the same condition as when purchased, minus the tags
- Clean and ready to wear/use

**When to use it:** The item was never worn or used, but you took the tags off (maybe you planned to wear it and changed your mind, or bought it without tags).

### Very Good

The item was **barely used** and is in **excellent condition**. It looks almost new — you might not even notice it's been used unless you look very closely.

**What buyers expect:**
- Minimal signs of wear (if any)
- No stains, tears, or damage
- Still looks and feels like a near-new item

**When to use it:** You wore or used the item once or twice, or it's been gently used but still looks great. There are no visible flaws that would catch someone's eye.

### Good

The item has been **used with care** and shows **some signs of wear**. It's in solid condition but clearly not new.

**What buyers expect:**
- Minor signs of wear (light pilling, slight fading, small scratches)
- No major flaws, tears, or holes
- Still wearable and functional

**When to use it:** You've worn or used the item regularly but took good care of it. There may be minor signs of wear that are visible on close inspection.

### Satisfactory

The item has been **well-used** and shows **visible wear**. It may need minor repair or cleaning. It's still functional but shows clear signs of its history.

**What buyers expect:**
- Visible signs of wear (pilling, fading, scratches, scuffs)
- May have minor flaws (small stain, loose thread, slight damage)
- Still wearable/usable but clearly used

**When to use it:** The item has been used frequently and shows it. Be specific in your description about what the wear looks like. Include photos of any flaws.

## How to choose the right condition

Follow this simple rule: **when in doubt, go one level lower.** It's always better for a buyer to receive an item that's slightly better than expected than slightly worse.

## What to include in your description

The condition level gives buyers a general idea, but your **description** and **photos** fill in the details. Here's what to mention for each level:

- **New with Tags** — Tag details, original packaging, where you bought it
- **New without Tags** — Why tags are missing, that it was never worn
- **Very Good** — Any tiny details that prevent it from being "New"
- **Good** — Specific signs of wear (where, what kind)
- **Satisfactory** — Every flaw in detail — stains, damage, wear areas
**The lower the condition, the more detail you should provide.** A "Satisfactory" listing needs thorough descriptions and close-up photos of every flaw. A "New with Tags" listing speaks mostly for itself.

## Photos and condition

For every condition level, include photos that support your rating:

- **New with Tags** — show the tags clearly in at least one photo
- **New without Tags** — show the item from multiple angles to confirm there's no wear
- **Very Good** — show the item overall and any tiny imperfections
- **Good** — include close-ups of any areas with wear
- **Satisfactory** — photograph every flaw: stains, damage, wear areas, loose threads. The more transparent you are, the fewer disputes you'll have

## Important tips

- **Be honest above everything.** Over-rating condition is the number one cause of returns on Jamble. A buyer who receives a "Good" item marked as "Very Good" will likely open a dispute
- **When in doubt, go lower.** Listing a "Very Good" item as "Good" means the buyer gets a pleasant surprise. Listing a "Good" item as "Very Good" means a complaint
- **Show every flaw in photos.** If there's a stain, scratch, or tear, photograph it. Buyers appreciate transparency and are more likely to buy (and keep) items when they know exactly what they're getting
- **Use your description wisely.** You have 120 characters — use them to mention condition details that photos can't fully show (material feel, smell, internal wear)
- **Different categories, same rules.** Whether you're selling sneakers, bags, trading cards, or accessories, the same five condition levels apply. Adapt your descriptions to what matters for each type of item

## Common questions

**Is condition required when listing a product?**
Condition is optional but strongly recommended. Products with a condition rating sell better because buyers know what to expect.

**Can I change the condition after listing?**
Yes. You can edit the condition on any listing before it sells. Tap the listing, update the condition, and save.

**What happens if a buyer says the condition doesn't match?**
The buyer can open a dispute. Jamble's support team will review the listing (photos, description, condition) and the item received. If the condition was misrepresented, the buyer may get a full refund. This is why accuracy matters.

**Does condition affect the price I can charge?**
Not directly — you set whatever price you want. But buyers naturally expect lower prices for lower conditions. A "Satisfactory" item priced like a "New with Tags" item will be hard to sell.

**What about collectible items like trading cards?**
The same five condition levels apply. For cards specifically, "New with Tags" means factory sealed or pack fresh, "Very Good" means near mint with no visible damage, and so on. Include close-up photos of the card's corners, edges, and surface.

**I bought an item online and it arrived without tags. Is it "New with Tags" or "New without Tags"?**
If it arrived without tags, list it as "New without Tags" — even if you never wore it. The buyer needs to know that tags are not attached.
