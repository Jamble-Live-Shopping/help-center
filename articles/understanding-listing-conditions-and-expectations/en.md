# Understanding listing conditions

## What you'll learn

When you create a listing, Jamble asks for the **condition** of your item. This guide explains the five conditions available today, what each one means, and how to pick the right one.

## Before you start

You just need the item in hand. Condition is part of the standard listing flow on Jamble: the field appears between **Color** and **Price**, and you pick one of five options.

## Where condition appears in the listing

When you create a listing, the app shows a **Condition** row in the product information list. Tapping that row opens a screen with the five available conditions.

![Condition row inside the create-listing flow, sitting between Color and Price](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Jamble-Live-Shopping/help-center/main/assets/mockups/understanding-listing-conditions-and-expectations__section-row__en__v3.png)

The **Condition** row shows the selected condition on the right side. Tap it to open the selection screen.

## The five available conditions

The **Condition** screen lists exactly five options, in the order they appear in the app. Each option has a title and a short description.

![Condition picker screen with five options: New with tags, New without tags, Very Good, Good, Satisfactory](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Jamble-Live-Shopping/help-center/main/assets/mockups/understanding-listing-conditions-and-expectations__condition-picker__en__v3.png)

Tap the option that best describes your item and confirm. The description below each title is the exact text the buyer will also see when they look at your listing.

### New with tags

**Unworn and pristine, complete with original purchase tags.**

Use this when the item has never been worn and still has the original tags attached. If you removed the tags but never wore the item, use **New without tags**.

### New without tags

**Never worn, in perfect condition, but without the original tags.**

Use this when the item has never been worn but the tags are no longer attached. If it arrived from the store without tags, use this option anyway: the buyer needs to know.

### Very Good

**Lightly used with minimal signs of wear, well-maintained.**

Use this when the item has been used but still looks nearly perfect. No stains, no tears, no visible flaws on a normal look.

### Good

**Gently worn and well cared for, with any defects clearly noted.**

Use this when there are light signs of use (small scratches, slight fading, minor pilling on fabric). No major flaws. List every visible defect in the description or show it in photos.

### Satisfactory

**Frequently worn with visible wear and noted defects.**

Use this when the wear is obvious. There may be small stains, marks, or worn areas. Photograph every defect up close and describe everything in the listing text.

## How to pick the right condition

Follow a simple rule: **when in doubt, go one level lower**. It's better for the buyer to receive an item that's slightly better than expected than slightly worse.

- Use **New with tags** only if the tags are still attached
- Use **New without tags** if it was never worn but the tags came off
- Use **Very Good** if the item was lightly used and still looks nearly perfect
- Use **Good** if there are light signs of use but no major flaws
- Use **Satisfactory** if there's visible wear or small defects

## What to write in the description

The condition is just the title. The listing **description** (up to 120 characters) and the **photos** fill in the details. The lower the condition, the more detail you should provide:

- **New with tags** and **New without tags**: confirm it was never worn, show the tags (or their absence) in a photo
- **Very Good**: point out any tiny imperfection that prevents the item from being "new"
- **Good**: describe where the wear is and what kind (scratch, light stain, pilling)
- **Satisfactory**: list every visible defect, photograph each one up close

## Editing the condition after listing

You can change the condition on a listing before it sells. Open the listing and use the **Edit** button to go back to the form.

## Important tips

- **Be accurate.** Buyers make their purchase decision by looking at your photos and the condition you picked. If the item arrives different, they can reach out to support
- **Show the defects.** If there's a stain, scratch, or tear, photograph it. Transparency reduces questions and returns
- **Use the description as a complement.** The official condition descriptions are short. Your listing description fills in the details photos can't capture (material feel, smell, internal wear)
- **Same options across all categories.** Whether sneakers, bags, trading cards, or accessories, the same five conditions apply

## Common questions

**How many conditions are there?**
Five: New with tags, New without tags, Very Good, Good, Satisfactory. The list always appears in the same order on the selection screen.

**Can I change the condition after listing?**
Yes, as long as the listing hasn't sold. Open the listing, tap **Edit**, and go back to the form.

**What happens if the buyer says the condition doesn't match?**
The buyer can reach out to support. The more accurate your condition, description, and photos, the easier it is to resolve any question.

**I bought an item online and it arrived without tags. What should I list it as?**
List it as **New without tags**, even if you never wore it. The buyer needs to know that the tags are not attached.

**Is there a condition for factory-sealed items?**
Use **New with tags**. The official description includes "unworn and pristine, complete with original purchase tags," which covers factory-sealed packaging.
