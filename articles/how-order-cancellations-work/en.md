# How Order Cancellations Work

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how orders can be cancelled on Jamble — when a buyer cancels, when you cancel as a seller, and what happens automatically if you don't ship in time.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- An active order from a buyer

## How cancellation works on Jamble

There are three ways an order can be cancelled:

1. **Buyer cancels directly** — the buyer can cancel before you confirm the sale
2. **You cancel as the seller** — you cancel the order yourself (item lost, damaged, buyer asked you to, etc.)
3. **Automatic cancellation** — the system cancels if you don't confirm or ship within the deadline

## When a buyer cancels

Buyers can cancel an order **only before you confirm the sale** — while the order is still in "Created" status.

If a buyer cancels at this stage:
- The order is closed automatically
- The buyer receives a full refund
- You don't need to do anything — the cancellation happens without your involvement
- There is no "accept or decline" step. The buyer's cancellation is immediate

**Once you confirm a sale, the buyer can no longer cancel directly.** If the buyer wants to cancel after you've confirmed, they need to contact you through the app chat, and you can cancel on their behalf using the "Buyer requested cancellation" reason.

## When you need to cancel as a seller

If you need to cancel an order yourself (item lost, damaged, or unable to ship), here's how:

### Step 1: Open the order

Go to your sales and find the order you need to cancel.

### Step 2: Start the cancellation

Tap the cancel option. You'll see a screen titled **"What's making you cancel?"**

### Step 3: Select a reason and submit

Choose the reason that best describes your situation and tap **Submit**.

A confirmation alert appears:

### Step 4: Wait for review

After submitting, the Jamble team reviews your cancellation request within **2 hours**. Once approved, the buyer is refunded and the order is closed.

## Automatic cancellations

The system automatically cancels orders in these situations:

| Situation | Deadline | What happens |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| You don't confirm a new sale | 3 days | Order auto-cancelled, buyer refunded |
| You confirm but don't ship | 10 days | Order moves to "Ship Timeout" status |
| Ship Timeout + grace period | 5 additional days | Order auto-refunded if still not shipped |
| Buyer doesn't confirm receipt after delivery | 3 days | Order auto-completed (not cancelled — you get paid) |

**The most important deadline is the 10-day shipping window.** After you confirm a sale, you have 10 days to generate a label and ship the package. If you miss this deadline, the order may be automatically cancelled and the buyer refunded.

## Important tips

- **Confirm sales quickly.** You have 3 days, but don't wait — buyers are excited about their purchase
- **Ship within 10 days.** This is the hard deadline. Missing it triggers automatic cancellation
- **If a buyer asks you to cancel after confirmation, use the "Buyer requested cancellation" reason.** This makes it clear the cancellation wasn't your fault
- **Repeated cancellations have consequences.** Abusive or excessive cancellations may lead to temporary restrictions or account suspension
- **Communicate with the buyer.** If there's a delay, message the buyer through the app chat. Transparency prevents disputes

## Common questions

**Can a buyer cancel after I've confirmed the sale?**
No. Once you confirm, the buyer can't cancel directly. They can ask you to cancel on their behalf, or they can contact support. If you agree to cancel, use the "Buyer requested cancellation" reason.

**What happens to my earnings when an order is cancelled?**
The buyer is refunded. No commission is charged on cancelled orders — the transaction is fully reversed.

**Will cancellations hurt my seller reputation?**
It depends on the reason. Cancellations with the "Buyer requested" reason have less impact. Cancellations due to "Unable to ship" or "Item lost" are more serious if they happen frequently.

**Can I cancel an order after it's been shipped?**
Once a package is in transit, cancellation is complex. Contact support — they can help coordinate with the buyer for a return if needed.

**What's the 2-hour review for?**
When you cancel an order, the Jamble team reviews it to ensure everything is handled properly — the buyer gets refunded, the label is voided if applicable, and the order is closed cleanly.

**What happens if I never confirm a sale?**
After 3 days, the order is automatically cancelled and the buyer is refunded. This counts negatively against your seller reputation.

**How many cancellations are too many?**
There's no fixed number, but the system monitors cancellation patterns. Frequent cancellations may result in warnings and eventually temporary restrictions.
