# Discounts During Your Show — Flash Sales

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how to offer discounts during your live show on Jamble using the Flash Sale feature. Flash Sales let you sell products at a discounted price with a countdown timer, creating urgency and excitement for your audience.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- An active live show
- Products listed with the **Buy It Now** sell mode

## What is a Flash Sale?

A Flash Sale is a timed discount on a Buy It Now product during your live show. Instead of selling at the full price, you offer the product at a percentage off — and the deal only lasts while the timer is running.

Flash Sales create urgency: buyers know the discount disappears when the timer hits zero, so they act fast.

## How Flash Sales work

Flash Sales are a variant of Buy It Now. When you start a sale on a Buy It Now product, you're given the choice to make it a regular Buy It Now or a Flash Sale.

Key differences:

| Feature | Buy It Now | Flash Sale |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Price | Full price you set | Discounted price (percentage off) |
| Timer | Standard sale timer | Countdown timer — deal ends when timer hits zero |
| Color | Blue theme | Green theme |
| Urgency | Normal | High — buyers know the deal is time-limited |

## Step by step

### Step 1: Start a sale on a Buy It Now product

During your live show, tap on a product that is set to the **Buy It Now** sell mode to start a sale.

You'll see a prompt asking how you want to sell this item:

![Flash sale mode picker showing three options: Flash Sale, Buy It Now, and Later](./assets/mockups/discounts-during-your-show-flash-sales__flash-sale-mode-picker__en.png)

### Step 2: Select Flash Sale

Tap **"Flash Sale"** to start the sale with a discount.

The Flash Sale activates with the discount percentage you configured when listing the product. The product banner turns **green** to signal a Flash Sale to your audience.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  ┌─────────────────────────┐    │
│  │ 🟢 Flash Sale  -20%    │    │
│  │ R$ 80,00 (was R$ 100)  │    │
│  │ Timer: 0:45             │    │
│  └─────────────────────────┘    │
│                                 │
│  [Product image]                │
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Step 3: Your audience buys

Buyers see the discounted price and the countdown timer. They can purchase the product immediately at the Flash Sale price by swiping to buy.

When the timer runs out, the Flash Sale ends. If there are remaining units, the sale closes.

## Setting up Flash Sale on a product

The Flash Sale discount percentage is configured when you list the product:

1. When creating or editing a product, set the sell mode to **Buy It Now**
2. Enable the Flash Sale option
3. Set the **discount percentage** (e.g., 10%, 20%, 30%)

**Limits:**
- Discount percentage: minimum **10%**, maximum **90%**
- Timer duration: minimum **5 seconds**, maximum **300 seconds** (5 minutes)

The discounted price is calculated automatically from your original price. For example, if your product is R$ 100 and you set a 20% Flash Sale discount, the Flash Sale price will be R$ 80.

## Important tips

- **Flash Sales work best for popular items.** Use them on products that already have buyer interest — the discount + timer creates a buying frenzy
- **Don't overuse Flash Sales.** If everything is a Flash Sale, the urgency effect wears off. Use them strategically for key products
- **Keep your camera on the product.** When a Flash Sale is active, talk about the product, show its details, and remind your audience the timer is counting down
- **Set a reasonable discount.** A 10-20% discount is usually enough to drive action. Going too high cuts into your earnings
- **Flash Sales only work with Buy It Now.** You can't Flash Sale a Real-time offers or Sudden Death product — those already have competition built in
- **You can't pin another product during a Flash Sale.** While a Flash Sale is running, you need to wait for it to end before starting a sale on another product

## Common questions

**Can I set different discount percentages for different products?**
Yes. Each product has its own Flash Sale percentage configured in the listing settings. You can set 10% on one product and 30% on another.

**What happens if no one buys during the Flash Sale?**
The sale ends when the timer runs out. The product returns to your inventory unsold. You can start another sale on it later.

**Can I cancel a Flash Sale once it's started?**
You can end the sale early from the host interface. The Flash Sale closes and no more purchases can be made.

**Is the 14% commission calculated on the discounted or original price?**
The commission is calculated on the price the buyer actually pays — the discounted Flash Sale price.

**Can I run a Flash Sale on the same product multiple times?**
Yes. If the product didn't sell or has multiple units, you can start a new Flash Sale on it during the same or future shows.

**Do buyers see both the original and discounted price?**
Yes. The Flash Sale banner shows the discounted price along with the original price, so buyers can see exactly how much they're saving.
