# How Real-time Offers Work

## What you'll learn

Real-time offers are the heart of live selling on Jamble. When you start a product sale during your show, buyers compete by placing offers in real time. This guide explains exactly how the system works — from setting up the sale to what happens when the timer ends.

## Before you start

- You need an approved seller account on Jamble
- You need a live show running (or scheduled)
- You need at least one product listed in your show

## The three sell modes

When you list a product on Jamble, you choose one of three sell modes:

- ****Real-time offers**** — Buyers compete by placing offers. Timer extends when new offers come in. Highest offer wins
- ****Sudden Death**** — Same as real-time offers, but the timer does NOT extend. When time runs out, it's over — no second chances
- ****Buy It Now**** — Fixed price. Buyers purchase instantly at the price you set. No competition
This article focuses on **Real-time offers** and **Sudden Death** — the two competitive sell modes.

## How a real-time offer sale works

### Step 1: Set up the product

When creating or editing a product for your show, select **"Real-time offers"** (or **"Sudden Death"**) as the sell mode.

Configure two key settings:

- **Starting price** — the minimum amount for the first offer (minimum R$ 5.00)
- **Duration** — how long the sale lasts (5 to 90 seconds)

![Sell Mode screen with Real-time offers selected, showing price and duration inputs](./assets/mockups/how-real-time-offers-work__sell-mode-picker__en.png)

### Step 2: Start the sale during your show

When you're live, find the product in your product list and tap **"Start"**. Here's what happens:

1. The product appears on screen for all viewers
2. The countdown timer begins
3. Buyers can start placing offers

![real-time-offer-card: Nike Air Max 90 product with current offer R$45, countdown timer 0:18, and Place offer R$50 button](./assets/mockups/how-real-time-offers-work__real-time-offer-card__en.png)

### Step 3: Buyers place offers

Buyers place offers above the current price. Each new offer becomes the leading offer. Multiple buyers can compete, driving the price up.

### Step 4: Timer behavior (the key difference)

**Real-time offers:** Every time a new offer is placed near the end of the timer, the timer **extends** by a few seconds. This gives other buyers a chance to respond. The sale doesn't end until the timer runs out with no new offers.

**Sudden Death:** The timer counts down without any extensions. When it hits zero, it's over. If a buyer places an offer at the last second, no extra time is added. This creates urgency and faster sales.

### Step 5: Sale ends

When the timer reaches zero:

- **Highest offer wins** — the buyer who placed the highest offer gets the product
- **Payment is processed** — the winning buyer is charged automatically
- **The sale appears** in your orders list like any other sale
- **If no one placed an offer** — the product is unsold and can be restarted later

## Pre-offers

For Real-time offers and Sudden Death products, you can enable **pre-offers**. This lets buyers place offers on your product **before** your show starts.

**How it works:**
- When you list a product with pre-offers enabled, buyers can see it in your upcoming show and place an offer in advance
- When you start the sale during your show, pre-offers become the opening offers
- The live audience can then compete against pre-offers by placing higher offers

**Important:** Pre-offers are only available when the product **quantity is 1**.

## Real-time offers vs Sudden Death: which to choose?

| Feature | Real-time offers | Sudden Death |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| Timer extends on new offers | Yes | No |
| Creates higher prices | Usually, because buyers have more time to react | Sometimes lower, because the timer doesn't extend |
| Sale duration | Can extend beyond initial timer | Fixed — always ends at the set time |
| Best for | High-value items where you want max price | Fast-paced sales, clearing inventory quickly |
| Pre-offers | Yes (qty = 1) | Yes (qty = 1) |

## Important tips

- **Start with a low starting price.** A low starting price (like R$ 5.00) attracts more offers. Buyers feel like they're getting a deal, and competition drives the final price up
- **Talk while the timer runs.** Don't just start the sale and stay silent. Describe the product, build excitement, encourage viewers to place offers. "Going once... who else wants this?"
- **Use Sudden Death for variety.** If you've been running Real-time offers all show, switch to Sudden Death for a few products. It changes the energy and keeps buyers engaged
- **Watch your timer setting.** 30 seconds is a good starting point. Too short (5 seconds) and buyers don't have time to react. Too long (90 seconds) and viewers lose interest
- **Pre-offers build anticipation.** Enable pre-offers for your best products. Buyers who place pre-offers are likely to show up for your live and compete
- **Pay attention to the starting price.** Even though low starting prices attract more offers, make sure you're comfortable selling at that price in case only one person places an offer

## Common questions

**What's the minimum starting price?**
R$ 5.00 for both Real-time offers and Sudden Death.

**What's the timer range?**
Minimum 5 seconds, maximum 90 seconds. We recommend 30 seconds as a starting point.

**Can I cancel a sale after it starts?**
Yes. You can cancel an active sale during your show. The product goes back to your product list and can be restarted.

**What happens if a buyer's payment fails?**
If the winning buyer's payment doesn't go through, the sale may be canceled. The product can then be restarted.

**Can I run multiple sales at the same time?**
Yes. You can have multiple products running simultaneously during your show — for example, one Real-time offers product and one Buy It Now product.

**Is there a commission on real-time offer sales?**
Yes. Jamble charges a 14% commission on all sales, regardless of the sell mode. This is calculated on the final sale price.

**Can I restrict who can place offers?**
Yes. When setting up a sale, you can choose the target audience: all buyers, only your followers, or only past buyers.
