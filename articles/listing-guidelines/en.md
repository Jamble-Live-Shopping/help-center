# Listing Guidelines

## What you'll learn

This guide explains the rules and expectations for listing products on Jamble. You'll learn what makes a good listing, what to avoid, and how to make sure your products meet the platform's standards.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Products you want to list

## What Jamble expects from your listings

When you list a product on Jamble, you're making a promise to potential buyers: this is what I'm selling, this is its condition, and this is what you'll receive. Jamble expects every listing to be honest, accurate, and fair.

Good listings build trust. Trust leads to sales, repeat buyers, and a positive reputation. Poor listings lead to disputes, returns, and potentially consequences for your account.

## Title guidelines

Your title has a maximum of **60 characters**. Make every character count.

**Do:**
- Be specific and descriptive: "Nike Air Max 90 - Size 42 - White"
- Include the brand, item type, size, and color when possible
- Use the actual product name as it appears on the tag or packaging

**Don't:**
- Use vague titles: "Nice shoes" or "Item for sale"
- Use ALL CAPS for the entire title
- Stuff keywords: "Nike Adidas Puma Jordan sneakers shoes"
- Use misleading terms: "100% authentic" when you can't verify it
- Add promotional language: "BEST DEAL!!!" or "MUST BUY!!!"

## Photo guidelines

You can add up to **10 photos** per listing. While photos are optional, they significantly impact whether buyers will purchase your item.

**Do:**
- Use clear, well-lit photos (natural light is best)
- Show the actual item you're selling — front, back, sides, and details
- Include close-ups of tags, brand markings, and any flaws
- Show the item on a clean, simple background

**Don't:**
- Use stock photos or images from the internet — always photograph the actual item
- Use heavily filtered or edited photos that misrepresent the item's appearance
- Include photos that are blurry, dark, or hard to see
- Show items that aren't part of the listing (unless clearly labeled "not included")

## Description guidelines

Descriptions are limited to **120 characters**. Use this space wisely.

**Do:**
- Mention condition details the photos can't show (material, feel, smell)
- Note any flaws, damage, or signs of wear
- Include relevant details: material, fit (true to size, runs large), edition info for collectibles

**Don't:**
- Copy descriptions from other sellers or websites
- Use the description for promotional language ("best item ever!")
- Leave the description empty if the item has any wear or flaws — buyers need this information

## Condition accuracy

Jamble has 5 condition levels: New with Tags, New without Tags, Very Good, Good, and Satisfactory. See [Understanding Listing Conditions](2-listing-conditions-expectations.md) for detailed guidance on each level.

The golden rule: **if in doubt, rate one level lower.** A buyer who receives a "Good" item rated as "Good" is happy. A buyer who receives a "Good" item rated as "Very Good" is disappointed — and may open a dispute.

## Pricing guidelines

- Minimum price: **R$ 5.00**
- Maximum price: **R$ 5,000.00**

Set your price based on the item's condition, brand, rarity, and what similar items sell for. There's no rule against pricing high, but unrealistic prices lead to no sales.

If you add a **retail price**, make sure it's the actual retail price of the item — not an inflated number to make the discount look bigger.

## Category and attribute accuracy

When selecting category, size, color, and other attributes:

- Choose the **correct gender** (Men or Women)
- Select the **accurate category and subcategory** — a sneaker should be listed under Shoes → Sneakers, not Clothes → Others
- Pick the **right size** — list the actual size on the tag, not the size you think it fits like
- Select the **accurate color** — match it to the dominant color of the item. Use "Multi" for multicolored items

Misclassified products confuse buyers and may be flagged by the platform.

## What you cannot list

Jamble is a fashion and lifestyle marketplace. Listings must be for physical products that can be shipped via Correios. The following are not allowed:

- **Counterfeit or fake items** — items that imitate a brand without being genuine
- **Stolen goods** — items you don't have the legal right to sell
- **Illegal items** — anything prohibited by Brazilian law
- **Weapons, drugs, or hazardous materials**
- **Items not matching their listing** — intentionally listing one item and shipping another
- **Empty listings** — listings with no actual product (every listing must represent a real item you have in hand)
- **Digital-only items** — everything sold on Jamble must be a physical product that ships

If you're unsure whether an item is allowed, contact support before listing it.

## What happens if a listing breaks the rules

When a listing is reported by a buyer or flagged by the Jamble team:

1. The listing (and sometimes the show) may be **removed from search results**
2. You may receive a **warning** from the support team
3. For repeated or serious violations, your **selling privileges may be restricted**

Jamble's operations team reviews reports and takes action to keep the marketplace safe for everyone.

## Important tips

- **List only items you have in hand.** Don't list items you plan to buy, items in transit, or items you don't physically possess. Buyers expect fast shipping
- **One listing per item.** Don't create duplicate listings for the same item across different shows. Use the Clone feature to move products between shows instead
- **Keep your listings updated.** If something about your item changes (you discover a flaw, packaging is damaged), update the listing before selling
- **Respond to buyer concerns.** If a buyer asks about condition or details during your show, be honest and specific
- **Build your reputation.** Good listings, accurate descriptions, and honest conditions lead to good reviews. Good reviews lead to more buyers. It's a cycle that starts with your listings

## Common questions

**Can Jamble remove my listing?**
Yes. If a listing violates the guidelines, it may be removed from search results or taken down. You'll typically receive a notification explaining why.

**What happens if a buyer reports my listing as inaccurate?**
The support team reviews the report. If the listing is found to be misleading (wrong condition, misleading photos, etc.), the buyer may receive a refund and your account may receive a warning.

**Can I relist an item that was removed?**
It depends on why it was removed. If it was a mistake (wrong category, for example), you can fix it and relist. If the item itself is prohibited, no.

**Are there specific rules for collectibles (trading cards, etc.)?**
The same general guidelines apply. For trading cards, be especially precise about condition — card condition is critical for buyers. Include clear, close-up photos of corners, edges, and surfaces.

**Can I sell items from other countries (imported)?**
Yes, as long as the item is currently in Brazil and can be shipped via Correios. Mentioning that an item is imported is helpful for buyers.

**Is there a minimum number of photos required?**
No. Photos are optional. But listings with photos sell significantly better than those without. We strongly recommend at least 3-4 photos showing the item from different angles.
