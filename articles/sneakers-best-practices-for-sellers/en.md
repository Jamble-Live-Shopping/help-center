# Sneakers: Best Practices for Sellers

## What you'll learn

This guide covers best practices for selling sneakers on Jamble in Brazil. You'll learn how to properly describe condition, take great photos, and set up your sneaker listings to build buyer trust and avoid returns.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Sneakers to sell with clear knowledge of their condition
- Good lighting for photos

## Authenticity

Selling counterfeit or fake products on Jamble is **strictly prohibited**. This includes:

- Replica sneakers ("inspired by" or "similar to" a brand)
- Items with unauthorized logos or trademarks
- Products listed as authentic when they are not

If you are not 100% sure a sneaker is authentic, do not sell it on Jamble. Selling counterfeit goods can lead to account suspension or permanent ban.

**How to protect yourself:**
- Buy from authorized retailers or trusted resellers
- Keep receipts and proof of purchase
- If a buyer questions authenticity, provide documentation
- When in doubt, disclose: "I purchased this secondhand and cannot guarantee authenticity" is honest and fair

## Condition guidelines

When listing sneakers, you must accurately describe the condition. Jamble uses these condition levels:

| Condition | What it means for sneakers |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **New with Tags** | Brand new, never worn, in original box with all tags and accessories (extra laces, etc.) |
| **New without Tags** | Brand new, never worn, but may be missing the original box, tags, or accessories |
| **Very Good** | Worn 1-2 times, barely visible signs of use, no significant marks or sole wear |
| **Good** | Gently used, some visible signs of wear on soles and upper, but still in solid shape |
| **Satisfactory** | Well-worn, visible creasing, sole wear, scuffs, or discoloration. Still wearable |

### Flaws you must disclose

Regardless of condition, you must mention any of these flaws if present:

**For New / New without Tags:**
- Missing accessories (extra laces, special packaging)
- Box damage or missing parts (no lid, replacement box)
- Any yellowing or discoloration
- Scuff marks, cuts, or paint chips
- Any separation on the shoe

**For Used sneakers (Very Good, Good, Satisfactory):**
- Major yellowing or discoloration
- Missing or replaced insoles
- Heavy sole wear or dirtiness
- Major scuff marks or cuts
- Any separation on the shoe
- Odor issues

**Be honest.** Over-describing the condition leads to returns and bad reviews. It's always better to slightly under-describe than to surprise the buyer with undisclosed flaws.

## Photo guidelines

Good photos are critical for sneakers. Here's what to include:

1. **Both shoes together** — front view, showing the full pair
2. **Side profile** — left and right side of each shoe
3. **Soles** — show the bottom of both shoes (wear is visible here)
4. **Back/heel** — shows the heel and back counter
5. **Box and tags** — if you have them, include a photo of the box label with size info
6. **Close-up of any flaws** — if there's yellowing, a scuff, or separation, photograph it up close

```
Recommended photo order (up to 10):
1. Both shoes — front view
2. Left shoe — side profile
3. Right shoe — side profile
4. Soles — both shoes
5. Back/heel view
6. Box label (if available)
7-10. Close-ups of details or flaws
```

**Tip:** Use natural lighting. Artificial light can change how colors look, which leads to buyer complaints about "wrong color."

## Listing tips for sneakers

### Title
Be specific. Include brand, model, colorway, and size.

**Good:** "Nike Air Max 90 - Tamanho 42 - Branco/Preto"
**Bad:** "Tênis Nike bonito"

### Size
Always specify the Brazilian size (numeração BR). If you know the US or EU equivalent, mention it in the description.

Common Brazilian sneaker sizes range from 34 to 47. Make sure the size in your listing matches the size on the shoe's label.

### Category
Select: **Shoes** → appropriate subcategory (Sneakers, Boots, etc.)

### Shipping profile
Most sneakers fit the **Bulkier Items** or **Standard Apparel** shipping profile, depending on whether you ship with the original box.

- With original box → **Bulkier Items**
- Without box → **Standard Apparel** or **Heavier Apparel**

## Selling sneakers during your show

- **Hold the shoe on camera.** Rotate it to show all angles. Buyers want to see the shoe in your hands, not just in photos
- **Mention the size clearly.** Say the size out loud multiple times
- **Point out condition details.** If there's a small scuff, show it on camera. Transparency builds trust
- **Know your product.** Be ready to answer questions about the model, release year, colorway, and retail price

## Common questions

**Do I need to authenticate sneakers before selling on Jamble?**
Jamble does not require third-party authentication. However, you must be confident that your sneakers are genuine. If a buyer files a complaint about authenticity, you may be held responsible.

**What size system should I use?**
Use Brazilian sizes (numeração BR). You can add US or EU equivalents in the description for reference.

**Should I ship sneakers in the original box?**
If you have it, yes. Sneakers in their original box are worth more and give buyers confidence. Make sure to package the box inside another shipping box to protect it during transit.

**What if a buyer says the sneakers are fake?**
Respond calmly and provide any proof of purchase you have. If you're confident the item is authentic, explain that to the buyer. If the dispute continues, contact Jamble support for help.

**How should I price my sneakers?**
Research what similar models sell for on Jamble and other platforms. Consider the condition, rarity, and current market demand. For popular models in good condition, Real-time offers often get a better price than Buy It Now.
