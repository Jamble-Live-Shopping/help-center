# Edit Your Shipping Settings

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how to edit your shipping settings after the initial setup. You'll learn how to change your shipping address, switch label formats, adjust your insurance preference, and choose between shipping speed options.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Your shipping settings already configured (if not, see the Getting Started guide on setting up shipping)

## Step by step

### Step 1: Open Shipping Preferences

Go to your profile, tap **Settings**, then tap **Shipping Preferences**.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  Settings                       │
│                                 │
│  Profile Settings               │
│  Payment Settings               │
│  ▶ Shipping Preferences         │
│  Notification Settings          │
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

This screen shows all your current shipping settings. Each setting has its own section that you can edit.

### Step 2: Edit your shipping address

Your shipping address determines where packages ship from and affects the shipping cost buyers see. To change it:

1. Find the address section in Shipping Preferences
2. Update your **CEP** (postal code) — the app will auto-fill the street, neighborhood, city, and state
3. Update your **street number** and **apartment/suite** if needed
4. Tap **Save**

**When to change your address:** If you move, or if you start shipping from a different location (like a business address instead of home).

### Step 3: Change your label format

You can switch between three label formats at any time:

| Format | Best for |
|--------|----------|
| **Half-page PDF** (default) | Regular home/office printers. Prints on half a sheet |
| **Full-page PDF** | Regular printers. Uses a full sheet of paper |
| **4×6 Thermal Label** | Thermal label printers (Zebra, etc.). Peel and stick |

To change:
1. Find the **Label Format** section
2. Select the format you want
3. Tap **Save**

The new format applies to all future labels. Labels you've already generated keep their original format.

### Step 4: Toggle package insurance

Package insurance adds a small cost to the shipping price but protects against lost or damaged packages.

To change:
1. Find the **Package Insurance** section
2. Toggle it on or off
3. Tap **Save**

**When to use insurance:** Insurance is applied to shipments where the total item value exceeds R$ 500. For lower-value items, the insurance toggle won't add extra cost. For high-value items, it's a good safety net against loss or damage.

## Important tips

- **Changes apply to future orders only.** Editing your settings doesn't affect orders that are already confirmed or labels that are already generated
- **Your address affects shipping costs.** If you change your address, the shipping cost for new orders will be recalculated based on your new location
- **You can change settings as often as you want.** There's no limit on how many times you can edit your preferences
- **Label format is a default, not a lock.** Your chosen format is used for all new labels, but you can adjust individual labels if needed

## Common questions

**Will changing my address affect orders I've already confirmed?**
No. Address changes only apply to future orders. Existing orders keep the address they were confirmed with.

**Can I use a different label format for a specific order?**
Your default label format applies to all new labels. If you need a different format for one order, change the setting before generating that label, then change it back.

**Does insurance cost me anything?**
No. The insurance cost is added to the shipping price that the buyer pays. It doesn't come out of your earnings. Insurance is only applied to shipments where the total value exceeds R$ 500.

**I changed my settings but my last label still has the old format. Why?**
Settings changes only apply to future labels. Labels that were already generated keep their original format. You can't retroactively change an existing label's format.
