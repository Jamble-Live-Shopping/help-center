# Edit Your Shipping Settings

## What you'll learn

This guide shows you how to edit your shipping settings after initial setup. You'll learn how to update your address, change your label format, and adjust your package insurance preference.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Your shipping settings already configured (if not yet, see the getting started guide on shipping setup)

## Step by step

### Step 1: Open Shipping Preferences

Go to your profile, tap **Settings**. In the **Sell** section, tap **Shipping Preferences**.

![Shipping Preferences screen with the Shipping Address row showing Av. Paulista 1578 and the Label section listing Label Format and Package Insurance](./assets/mockups/edit-your-shipping-settings__shipping-preferences__en__v2.png)

This screen brings together everything you can edit: address, label format, and package insurance.

### Step 2: Update your shipping address

Your shipping address defines where packages are sent from and affects the shipping cost the buyer sees at checkout. To change it:

1. Tap **Shipping Address**
2. Delete the old CEP and type the new one
3. The app automatically fills in street, neighborhood, city, and state
4. Update **number** and **apartment** if needed
5. Tap **Save**

![Shipping Address form with the CEP field in focus showing 04538-132 and street, number, neighborhood, city, and state filled in](./assets/mockups/edit-your-shipping-settings__shipping-address-edit__en__v2.png)

**When to change your address:** you've moved, or started shipping from another location (like a business address instead of home).

### Step 3: Change your label format

You can switch between three formats at any time:

- **Half-page PDF** (default). Any regular printer, half an A4 sheet
- **Full-page PDF**. Full A4 sheet, larger labels and easier to read
- **4x6 thermal label**. For thermal printers (Zebra or similar). Peel-and-stick, no cutting needed

To change:

1. Tap **Label Format**
2. Select the new format
3. Tap **Save**

![Label Format selector with three options: Half-page PDF, Full-page PDF, and 4x6 thermal label selected in purple highlight](./assets/mockups/edit-your-shipping-settings__label-format-picker__en__v2.png)

The new format applies to all labels generated from now on. Labels already generated keep their original format.

### Step 4: Enable or disable package insurance

Package insurance protects high-value shipments against loss or damage. The cost is added to the shipping fee the buyer pays, never deducted from your earnings.

To change:

1. Tap **Package Insurance**
2. Enable or disable it
3. Tap **Save**

**When to use insurance:** insurance is applied to shipments where the total value of items exceeds R$ 500. For lower-value items, the option adds no cost. For expensive items, it's important protection.

## Important tips

- **Changes only apply to future orders.** Editing doesn't affect orders already confirmed or labels already generated
- **Your address affects shipping cost.** When you change it, the shipping value for new orders is recalculated based on your new location
- **You can edit as many times as you want.** There's no limit
- **Test the new format.** After switching, generate a test label on your next order before committing to a large batch

## Frequently asked questions

**Does changing my address affect orders I've already confirmed?**
No. Address changes only apply to future orders. Existing orders keep the address they were confirmed with.

**Can I use a different format for a specific order?**
The chosen format applies to all new labels. If you need a different format for one order, change the setting before generating that label and then switch back to your previous format.

**Does insurance cost me anything?**
No. The insurance cost is added to the shipping fee the buyer pays. It doesn't come out of your earnings. Insurance is only applied to shipments with a total value above R$ 500.

**I changed my settings but my last label is still in the old format. Why?**
Changes only apply to future labels. Labels already generated keep their original format. You can't change the format of an existing label after it's been generated.
