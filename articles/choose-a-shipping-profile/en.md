# Choose a Shipping Profile

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how shipping profiles work on Jamble. You'll learn what each profile is designed for, how to choose the right one for your products, and how shipping profiles affect the cost your buyers pay.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Your shipping address configured (see [Set Up Shipping](../1-getting-started/4-set-up-shipping.md))

## What is a shipping profile?

A shipping profile is a **size and weight category** that you assign to each product you list. It tells Jamble how big and heavy the package will be, so the system can calculate the shipping cost for buyers.

You don't set shipping prices yourself. When a buyer purchases your product, Jamble automatically calculates the shipping cost based on three things:

1. **The shipping profile** you selected (size and weight of the package)
2. **Your address** (where the package ships from)
3. **The buyer's address** (where the package ships to)

The buyer sees and pays the shipping cost at checkout. It's added on top of the product price.

## The 11 shipping profiles

Jamble has 11 preset shipping profiles. You choose one every time you list a product.

### Card

**Best for:** Single trading cards, flat items, stickers

The smallest and lightest profile. Use it for items that fit in a standard envelope — a single Pokémon card, a flat sticker, or any item that's thin and light.

### Booster

**Best for:** Card packs, multiple cards, small sealed packs

Slightly larger than Card. Use it for booster packs, small groups of cards, or any small sealed product that's a bit bulkier than a single card.

### Light Accessories

**Best for:** Jewelry, scarves, small accessories

For lightweight accessories that don't take up much space. Think earrings, bracelets, thin scarves, phone cases, or other small accessories.

### Light Apparel

**Best for:** T-shirts, tank tops, blouses, thin tops

For lightweight clothing items. A single t-shirt, a tank top, a blouse — anything that folds flat and doesn't weigh much.

### Standard Apparel

**Best for:** Shirts, dresses, sweaters, regular clothing

The most common profile for clothing. Use it for items that are a bit heavier or bulkier than a simple t-shirt — button-down shirts, summer dresses, sweaters, or similar items.

### Heavier Apparel

**Best for:** Jeans, thick dresses, trousers, heavier garments

For clothing items that are notably heavier. Jeans, thick dresses, wool trousers, or multiple layered garments.

### Bulkier Items

**Best for:** Jackets, winter coats, heavy or bulky items

The largest single-item profile. Use it for bulky items like winter coats, puffy jackets, heavy boots, or anything that takes up significant space in a package.

### Small Bundles

**Best for:** 2-3 items shipped together

When you're selling a small group of items that ship in one package. For example, a set of 2-3 t-shirts or a small collection of accessories.

### Medium Bundles

**Best for:** 4-6 items shipped together

For medium-sized groups. A bundle of 4-6 clothing items, or a collection that fills a medium box.

### Large Bundles

**Best for:** 7+ items shipped together

For large groups of items. A big clothing haul, a large collection of cards, or any order that needs a large box.

### Extra-Large Bundles

**Best for:** Very large orders, big collections

The largest profile available. Use it for very large orders — big clothing collections, large lots of collectibles, or anything that needs the biggest packaging option.

## How to choose the right profile

Follow these steps:

1. **Think about the actual package** — not just the item. A thin scarf is "Light Accessories" but a scarf in a gift box might be "Light Apparel"
2. **Consider the weight** — heavier items need a larger profile even if they're small. Heavy boots are "Bulkier Items" even though they might fit in a medium box
3. **When in doubt, go one size up** — it's better for the shipping cost to be slightly higher than to struggle fitting your item into a too-small package

## How the profile affects the buyer

The shipping profile directly impacts:

- **Shipping cost at checkout** — a bigger profile means higher shipping. Buyers see the exact cost before they pay
- **Buyer's decision to purchase** — some buyers won't buy if shipping feels too high compared to the product price. Choosing an unnecessarily large profile costs the buyer more and can hurt your sales
- **Shipping experience** — if the profile is too small for the actual item, you may struggle to package and ship it properly, leading to delays or damaged items

## Can I create custom shipping profiles?

No. Jamble uses a fixed set of 11 profiles. You choose the one that best matches each product. These profiles are calibrated for the shipping costs provided by Correios (the Brazilian postal service).

## Changing a shipping profile

You can change the shipping profile on any listing **before it sells**. Just tap the listing, update the profile, and save.

If you realize after a sale that you chose the wrong profile, contact support — they can help adjust the shipping label.

## Important tips

- **Choose the profile BEFORE your show, not during.** Take the time to pick the right profile when you're listing products, not when you're live and rushing
- **The app remembers your last profile.** When you create a new product, Jamble pre-fills the shipping profile from your previous listing. This saves time when listing similar items — but double-check it's correct if your next item is a different size
- **Bundle profiles are for multiple items in one package.** If a single buyer buys 5 items from you, those might ship as a bundle. Choose the Bundle profile that matches the total package size
- **Clothing sellers:** most of your items will use Light Apparel, Standard Apparel, or Heavier Apparel. Get familiar with these three and you'll almost always pick the right one
- **Card/collectible sellers:** most of your items will use Card or Booster. Use Card for singles and Booster for packs or small lots

## Common questions

**Does the shipping profile affect my earnings?**
No. The buyer pays the shipping cost — it's added on top of the product price at checkout. Your earnings come from the product price minus Jamble's commission. Shipping cost never reduces your earnings.

**Can I change the shipping profile after selling?**
Not directly. Contact support if you need to adjust the shipping label after a sale.

**What happens if my package is heavier than the profile I chose?**
If the package is significantly heavier, the actual shipping cost may exceed what the buyer paid. To avoid this, always choose a profile that accurately matches your packaged item.

**Can I see what shipping cost the buyer will pay?**
You can't see the exact cost since it depends on the buyer's location. But the profile you choose is the main factor — a "Light Apparel" item shipping across Brazil costs less than a "Bulkier Items" package.

**All shipments go through Correios?**
Yes. All Jamble shipments in Brazil are handled by Correios. The system automatically selects the most economical shipping option for each order.

**Can buyers use credits to reduce the cost?**
Yes. If a buyer has credits (from referrals or promotions), they're automatically applied at checkout to reduce or cover the shipping cost.
