# Choose a Shipping Profile

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how shipping profiles work on Jamble. You'll learn what each profile is for, how to pick the right one, and how it affects the shipping your buyer pays.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- Your shipping address set up (see [Set Up Shipping](../1-getting-started/4-set-up-shipping.md))

## What is a shipping profile?

A shipping profile is a **size and weight category** that you assign to each product you list. It tells Jamble what the package size and weight will be, so the system can calculate the shipping cost for buyers.

You don't set the shipping prices. When a buyer purchases your product, Jamble automatically calculates the shipping cost based on three things:

1. **The shipping profile** you selected (package size and weight)
2. **Your address** (where the package ships from)
3. **The buyer's address** (where the package goes)

The buyer sees and pays the shipping cost at checkout. It's added to the product price.

## Quick guide: which profile to use?

Jamble has 11 pre-defined profiles. Choose the one that matches the size and weight of the actual package, not just the item.

**Single items**

- **Card**: single collectible cards, flat items, stickers. Fits in a standard envelope.
- **Booster**: card packs, multiple cards, small sealed products. Slightly larger than Card.
- **Light Accessories**: jewelry, earrings, bracelets, thin scarves, phone cases.
- **Light Apparel**: t-shirts, tank tops, blouses, thin tops. Anything that folds flat.
- **Standard Apparel**: button-up shirts, summer dresses, sweaters, regular clothing. The most common profile for apparel.
- **Heavier Apparel**: jeans, thick dresses, wool pants, layered pieces.
- **Bulkier Items**: jackets, winter coats, heavy boots. The largest profile for a single item.

**Multi-item bundles**

- **Small Bundles**: 2-3 items shipped together. For example, a t-shirt set.
- **Medium Bundles**: 4-6 items. Fills a medium box.
- **Large Bundles**: 7+ items. Large clothing or card lots.
- **Extra-Large Bundles**: very large orders, large collections. The largest profile available.

## How to choose the right profile

Three simple rules:

1. **Think about the actual package**, not just the item. A thin scarf is "Light Accessories", but a scarf in a gift box might be "Light Apparel".
2. **Consider the weight**. Heavier items need a larger profile even if they're small. Heavy boots are "Bulkier Items" even if they fit in a medium box.
3. **When in doubt, go one size up**. It's better for shipping to be slightly more expensive than to have trouble packing the item.

## How the profile affects the buyer

The shipping profile directly impacts:

- **Shipping cost at checkout**: a larger profile means more expensive shipping. Buyers see the exact amount before paying.
- **Purchase decision**: some buyers won't complete a purchase if shipping seems too high compared to the product price. Choosing too large a profile costs the buyer more and can hurt your sales.
- **Shipping experience**: if the profile is too small for the item, you may have difficulty packing and shipping correctly, causing delays or damaged items.

## Can I create custom profiles?

No. Jamble uses a fixed set of 11 profiles. You choose the one that best matches each product. These profiles are calibrated for Correios (Brazil's postal service) shipping costs.

## Change a shipping profile

You can change the profile of any listing **before it's sold**. Just tap the listing, update the profile, and save.

If you realize after a sale that you chose the wrong profile, contact support. The team can help adjust the shipping label.

## Important tips

- **Choose the profile BEFORE your show**, not during. Take time to select the right profile when listing products, not when you're live and in a rush.
- **The app remembers your last profile.** When you create a new product, Jamble fills in the profile from the previous listing. This saves time when listing similar items, but double-check it's correct if the next item is a different size.
- **Bundle profiles are for multiple items** in a single shipment. If a buyer takes 5 of your items, they can be sent as one bundle. Choose the Bundle profile that matches the total size.
- **Apparel sellers**: most of your items will use Light Apparel, Standard Apparel, or Heavier Apparel. Get familiar with these three and you'll almost always choose correctly.
- **Card and collectible sellers**: most of your items will use Card or Booster. Card for single items, Booster for packs or small lots.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the shipping profile affect my earnings?**
No. The buyer pays for shipping, which is added to the product price at checkout. Your earnings come from the product price minus Jamble's commission. Shipping cost never reduces your earnings.

**Can I change the profile after selling?**
Not directly. Contact support if you need to adjust the shipping label after a sale.

**What if my package is heavier than the profile I chose?**
If the package is significantly heavier, the actual shipping cost may exceed what the buyer paid. To avoid this, always choose a profile that matches your packed item.

**Can I see what shipping cost the buyer will pay?**
You can't see the exact amount, since it depends on the buyer's location. But the profile you choose is the main factor. A "Light Apparel" item shipped across Brazil costs less than a "Bulkier Items" bundle.

**Do all shipments go through Correios?**
Yes. All Jamble shipments in Brazil go through Correios (Brazil's postal service). The system automatically selects the most economical shipping option for each order.

**Can buyers use credits to reduce the cost?**
Yes. If a buyer has credits (from referrals or promotions), they're automatically applied at checkout to reduce or cover the shipping cost.
