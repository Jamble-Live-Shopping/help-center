# Set Up and Start a Payout

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how to register your bank details and withdraw your earnings from Jamble to your bank account via PIX.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- A Brazilian bank account in your name
- Your CPF (or CNPJ if you sell as a company)
- Available earnings in your wallet (at least one completed sale)

## Step by step

### Step 1: Open your wallet

Go to your profile, then tap **Settings**. Find and tap **My Wallet**.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  Settings                       │
│                                 │
│  SELL                           │
│  My Sales                       │
│  ▶ My Wallet                    │
│  Shipping Preferences           │
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

You can also access your wallet directly from your profile page.

### Step 2: Register your bank (first time only)

If this is your first time, you'll see a **Register Bank** button at the bottom of the wallet screen. Tap it.

```

│                                 │
│  [Wallet sections loading...]   │
│                                 │
│  ┌───────────────────────────┐  │
│  │      Register Bank        │  │
│  └───────────────────────────┘  │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

An alert appears:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  Registration                   │
│                                 │
│  You must first verify your     │
│  identity and bank details.     │
│  Jamble is powered by Pagar.me  │

│                                 │
│  [ Individual ]                 │
│  [ Company ]                    │
│  [ Later ]                      │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

Choose the option that matches your situation:
- **Individual** — if you sell as a person (using your CPF)
- **Company** — if you sell as a registered business (using your CNPJ)

This opens a registration form where you'll provide your identity and bank details. The form is powered by **Pagar.me**, Jamble's payment partner. Follow the steps in the form to complete your registration.

### Step 3: Check your bank details

After registration, your wallet screen shows your bank information in a **Bank Details** section at the bottom.

│  │       Withdraw            │  │
│  └───────────────────────────┘  │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

If you need to change your bank details, tap the **Update** button next to your bank info.

### Step 4: Withdraw your earnings

When you have available earnings in your wallet, the **Withdraw** button appears at the bottom of the screen. Tap it to request a payout.

That's it — one tap. Jamble sends your available balance to your registered bank account via bank transfer. The minimum withdrawal amount is **R$ 100**. Transfers typically take **2 to 5 business days** to arrive.

**Note:** You can only withdraw once every 24 hours. After a withdrawal, the button will show a countdown until your next withdrawal is available.

### Step 5: Track your payout

After requesting a withdrawal, you can track its progress. Tap the **clock icon** in the top-right corner of the wallet screen to open **Payouts History**.

```

Each payout shows:
- **Payout ID** — a unique reference number
- **Amount** — how much was withdrawn
- **Status** — where the payout is in the process
- **Date** — when the payout was requested

### Payout statuses

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| Created | Your payout request has been received |
| Processing | Pagar.me is processing the transfer to your bank |
| Pending | The transfer is waiting to be completed |
| Completed | The money has arrived in your bank account |
| Failed | Something went wrong (see troubleshooting article) |
| Canceled | The payout was canceled |

## Important tips

- **Register your bank BEFORE your first sale.** Don't wait until you have earnings — set up your bank details early so withdrawals are instant when you need them
- **Withdraw is one tap.** Tap "Withdraw" and your entire available balance is sent to your bank. Minimum withdrawal is R$ 100
- **Transfers take 2-5 business days.** After requesting a withdrawal, the bank transfer typically arrives within 2 to 5 business days. Check "Payouts History" for updates
- **One withdrawal per day.** You can withdraw once every 24 hours. Plan your withdrawals accordingly
- **Keep your bank details up to date.** If you change banks, update your bank details in the wallet before requesting a withdrawal. Withdrawals to incorrect bank details will fail
- **Individual vs Company matters.** Choose the correct type during registration. If you're a registered business (MEI, LTDA, etc.), select "Company" to use your CNPJ. Otherwise, select "Individual" for your CPF

## Common questions

**How do I receive my money?**
Via PIX. When you tap "Withdraw," Jamble transfers your available balance to your registered bank account using PIX.

**How long does a withdrawal take?**
Bank transfers typically take 2 to 5 business days to arrive in your account.

**Is there a minimum withdrawal amount?**
Yes. The minimum withdrawal is R$ 100. Your available balance must be at least R$ 100 to see the Withdraw button.

**How often can I withdraw?**
Once every 24 hours. After a withdrawal, you'll need to wait 24 hours before you can withdraw again.

**Can I withdraw to any bank?**
You can withdraw to any Brazilian bank account that accepts PIX and is registered in your name (matching your CPF or CNPJ).

**I don't see the Withdraw button. Why?**
Either you don't have available earnings yet (your orders may still be in progress), or you haven't registered your bank details. Check if the button says "Register Bank" instead — if so, complete the bank registration first.

**Can I change my bank account?**
Yes. Tap the **Update** button in the Bank Details section of your wallet to update your bank information.

**What happens if my payout fails?**
Check your bank details are correct. The most common cause of failed payouts is incorrect bank information. Update your details and try again. If the problem persists, contact support.
