# PIX Payments — How Sellers Get Paid

## What you'll learn

This guide explains how payments work for sellers on Jamble. You'll learn how your earnings are calculated, how to set up your bank account, how to request a payout via PIX, and how to troubleshoot payment issues.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- A Brazilian bank account (conta corrente or conta poupança)
- Your CPF (or CNPJ if selling as a company)

## How sellers get paid on Jamble

When a buyer purchases one of your products during a show, the money doesn't go directly to your bank account. Here's the flow:

1. **Buyer pays** — the buyer pays the full price (product + shipping) at checkout
2. **Jamble holds the funds** — the money is held securely while you ship the order
3. **You ship the order** — you generate a label, pack the item, and drop it off at a Correios agency
4. **Buyer receives the item** — the buyer confirms they received it (or the delivery is automatically confirmed)
5. **Earnings become available** — the money moves to your "available" balance in your wallet
6. **You request a payout** — you tap "Withdraw" and the money is sent to your bank via PIX

## Your earnings breakdown

Jamble charges a **14% commission** on each sale. This is deducted automatically before the money reaches your wallet.

**Example:**
- You sell an item for R$ 100
- Jamble's commission: R$ 14
- Your earnings: R$ 86

The buyer also pays for shipping separately — shipping costs are not deducted from your earnings.

## Step by step

### Step 1: Set up your bank account

Before you can receive any payouts, you need to register your bank details with Pagar.me (Jamble's payment processor).

Go to your profile, then tap **Settings**, then tap **My Wallet**.

If your bank account isn't set up yet, you'll see a **"Register Bank"** button at the bottom of the screen.

![Wallet-nav header showing My Wallet title with clock and help icons, and Register Bank button](./assets/mockups/pix-payments-how-sellers-get-paid__wallet-header__en.png)

Tap **"Register Bank"** and choose:
- **Individual** — if you're selling as a person (CPF)
- **Company** — if you're selling as a business (CNPJ)

This opens a registration form where you'll provide:
- Your identity information (name, CPF or CNPJ)
- Your bank details (bank name, branch, account number, account type)

Complete the form and submit. Once verified, your bank account is linked and ready to receive payouts.

### Step 2: Check your wallet

After making sales, go to **My Wallet** to see your earnings. The wallet shows different sections:

- **Available** — money you can withdraw right now
- **Waiting funds** — money from recent sales that hasn't been released yet (waiting for buyer confirmation)
- **Transferred** — total amount already sent to your bank

Your bank details are also shown at the bottom of the wallet screen. If you need to update them, tap the **"Update"** button next to your bank information.

### Step 3: Request a payout

When you have available funds, a **"Withdraw"** button appears at the bottom of the wallet screen.

![My Wallet screen showing available balance of R$ 250.00, waiting funds of R$ 120.00, transferred amount of R$ 1,500.00, bank details for Banco 260, and Withdraw button](./assets/mockups/pix-payments-how-sellers-get-paid__my-wallet__en.png)

Tap **"Withdraw"** to request a PIX payout. The full available balance is sent to your registered bank account.

PIX payouts are processed instantly in most cases. The money usually arrives in your bank account within minutes.

### Step 4: Check your payout history

To see all your past payouts, tap the **clock icon** (🕐) at the top right of the wallet screen. This opens the **"Payouts History"** screen.

Each payout shows:
- **Payout ID**
- **Amount** (in R$)
- **Status** (processing, completed, or failed)
- **Date**

## When your earnings become available

Your earnings don't become available instantly after a sale. Here's the timeline:

- **Buyer purchases your item** — Money is held as "waiting funds"
- **You ship the order** — Money remains in "waiting funds"
- **Buyer receives and confirms** — Money moves to "available"
- **Delivery confirmed automatically** — Money moves to "available" (if buyer doesn't confirm manually)
## Important tips

- **Set up your bank account before your first show.** This is the most common mistake new sellers make. If you sell without bank details, your earnings are held until you complete the setup
- **PIX payouts are instant.** Unlike wire transfers that take days, PIX sends money to your bank in minutes
- **Minimum withdrawal is R$ 100.** You need at least R$ 100 in available balance to request a payout
- **One withdrawal per day.** After requesting a payout, you need to wait **24 hours** before requesting another one
- **Keep your bank details up to date.** If you change banks, update your details in the wallet immediately. Failed payouts due to incorrect bank details will be returned to your wallet
- **Check your wallet regularly.** After shipping orders, monitor your wallet to see when funds move from "waiting" to "available"

## Common questions

**How long does a PIX payout take?**
PIX payouts are usually instant — the money arrives in your bank within minutes. In rare cases, it can take up to a few hours if there's a delay with the bank.

**Is there a minimum amount to withdraw?**
Yes. The minimum withdrawal is **R$ 100**. You need at least R$ 100 in available balance to request a payout.

**What if my payout fails?**
If a payout fails, the money returns to your wallet. Check your payout history for the reason. Common causes: incorrect bank details, closed account, or invalid CPF/CNPJ. Fix the issue and try again.

**Why are some funds in "waiting" status?**
Funds stay in "waiting" until the buyer confirms delivery of the item. This protects both you and the buyer. Once delivery is confirmed (manually by the buyer or automatically), the funds move to "available."

**How is the 14% commission calculated?**
The commission is 14% of the product sale price. Shipping costs are paid by the buyer and are not included in the commission calculation.

**Can I change my bank account?**
Yes. Go to My Wallet and tap the **"Update"** button next to your bank details. This opens the bank registration form where you can enter new details.

**Why does my wallet show "Register Bank" instead of "Withdraw"?**
Your bank details haven't been set up yet. Tap "Register Bank" to complete the Pagar.me registration and link your bank account.

**What happens if the buyer doesn't confirm delivery?**
If the buyer doesn't manually confirm delivery, the system will automatically confirm it after a set period once the tracking shows the package was delivered. Your funds will then move to "available."
