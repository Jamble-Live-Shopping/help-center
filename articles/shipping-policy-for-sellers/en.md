# Shipping Policy for Sellers

## What you'll learn

This guide covers the shipping rules for sellers on Jamble: the deadline to ship after a sale, how to generate the label, packaging requirements, tracking, and what happens if you ship late.

## Before you start

You need:
- An approved seller account on Jamble
- At least one confirmed sale from a show

## The flow in 4 steps

When a buyer purchases a product during your show, you are responsible for:

1. **Generating the shipping label** from the post-show dashboard
2. **Packing the item** properly to prevent damage during transit
3. **Dropping off the package** at a Correios agency
4. **Ensuring tracking** so the buyer can follow the order

All shipments on Jamble go through **Correios** (Brazil's national postal service). The label is generated in the app, via the integrated shipping service.

## Shipping deadline

After confirming a sale, you have **10 days** to drop the package off at Correios. Ship as early as possible, don't wait until the deadline.

| Step | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Confirm the sale | Within 3 days of the sale |
| Generate the label | As soon as possible after confirming |
| Pack and drop off the package | Within **10 days** of confirming the sale |
| Buyer receives the item | Per Correios delivery time (varies by distance) |

While the order is open, it appears as **Awaiting Shipment** on your dashboard, with a days-left countdown:

![Awaiting Shipment order card with Generate Label button and 3 days Left countdown](./assets/mockups/shipping-policy-for-sellers__shipping-deadline-card__en__v2.png)

If you don't ship within 10 days, the order moves to **Ship Timeout** status and may be automatically cancelled, with a full refund to the buyer.

## What the buyer pays

The buyer pays for shipping at checkout. The cost is calculated automatically based on:

- The **shipping profile** assigned to the product (package size and weight)
- Your **shipping address** (origin)
- The **buyer's address** (destination)
- Your **shipping speed setting** (cheapest or priority)
- Whether **package insurance** is enabled

You never pay shipping costs from your earnings. The buyer covers the full amount.

## How to generate the label

After your show, go to the post-show dashboard. Your sales appear organized by buyer, in bundles. For each bundle you can:

- **Generate one label**: creates a Correios label with a tracking code
- **Generate all labels at once**: processes every buyer in the show as a batch

Each label contains:
- Sender and recipient addresses
- A Correios tracking number and barcode
- A content declaration (when required)

You can choose the label format in **Settings > Shipping Preferences**:
- Half Page 8.5 x 11 (default)
- Full Page 8.5 x 11
- Thermal 4 x 6

## Packaging requirements

- **Use appropriate packaging.** The package must match the shipping profile you selected. A "Light Apparel" profile is not for a heavy jacket.
- **Protect the item.** Use bubble wrap, tissue paper, or other padding to prevent damage during transit.
- **Seal it securely.** Use tape so the package doesn't open during handling.
- **Attach the label clearly.** The barcode and addresses must be fully visible, without folds or obstructions.

## Tracking

Every label generated through Jamble includes a tracking number. Both you and the buyer can follow the shipment.

- Tracking is updated automatically as the package moves through the Correios network
- The buyer receives notifications when the status changes
- You can mark the package as shipped in the app after dropping it off at Correios

If you use your own external label (outside Jamble), add the tracking number to the order manually in the app. Without tracking, the buyer sees no progress and may open a complaint.

## What happens if you ship late

If the 10-day deadline passes without shipment, the system shows a warning and the order enters **Ship Timeout**:

![Shipping deadline missed alert with notice about automatic cancellation and buyer refund](./assets/mockups/shipping-policy-for-sellers__late-shipment-warning__en__v2.png)

Possible consequences:
- The buyer may request a cancellation
- The order may be automatically cancelled and refunded
- Your seller reputation is negatively affected
- Repeated late shipments may lead to warnings or account restrictions

## If the buyer says they didn't receive it

If the buyer tells you the item didn't arrive:

1. Check the tracking status in the app
2. If Correios shows it as delivered, send the tracking screenshot to the buyer through the app chat
3. If the package has been in transit for a long time or is lost, contact Jamble support: the team opens a review and can trigger insurance (when enabled)

Don't ignore the message. Transparency and a fast response resolve most cases without escalation.

## Seller-initiated cancellation

If you need to cancel an order (item lost, damaged, unable to ship), open the order in the app and select a reason:

- Unable to ship the item
- Item lost
- Item damaged
- Buyer requested cancellation
- Other reasons

**Warning:** repeated or abusive cancellations may lead to temporary restrictions or account suspension.

## Nota Fiscal (Invoice)

For sellers registered as a business (with CNPJ), Jamble supports **Nota Fiscal** generation:

- **Automatic generation**: if your digital certificate is registered, the system generates the invoice automatically
- **Manual addition**: you can attach a Nota Fiscal from the bundle detail
- **Exemption**: if you're not required to issue a Nota Fiscal, you can mark the bundle as exempt

Individual sellers (CPF only) are generally exempt.

## Important tips

- **Ship within the deadline.** This is the single most important rule. Late shipments hurt your reputation and frustrate buyers.
- **Choose the correct shipping profile.** An undersized profile means the actual shipping cost may exceed what the buyer paid.
- **Package items carefully.** Damaged items lead to returns, refunds, and bad reviews.
- **Keep your shipping address up to date.** If you move, update the address in **Settings** immediately, as it affects shipping cost calculations.
- **Communicate with the buyer if there's a delay.** A quick note through the app chat prevents complaints.

## Common questions

**Who pays for shipping?**
The buyer always pays for shipping. The cost is calculated automatically and added at checkout.

**Can I use a carrier other than Correios?**
For now, all Jamble shipments are processed through Correios. If you need to use another, generate your own label externally and add the tracking number manually to the order.

**How many days do I have to ship?**
You have **10 days** after confirming a sale. If you don't ship within this window, the order may be automatically cancelled and the buyer refunded.

**What if a buyer complains about high shipping costs?**
Shipping costs are calculated based on the shipping profile, distance, and speed. Make sure the profile is correct. An oversized profile increases costs unnecessarily. If costs seem wrong, contact support.

**What happens if I cancel too many orders?**
Repeated cancellations may result in temporary restrictions on your account. Only cancel when genuinely necessary.

**Do I need to issue a Nota Fiscal?**
If you sell as a business (with CNPJ) and meet certain criteria, you may need to issue a Nota Fiscal. The app will prompt you when needed. Individual sellers (CPF only) are generally exempt.
